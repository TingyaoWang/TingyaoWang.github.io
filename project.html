Data Cleaning

When creating the regression on the corruption index and GDP per capita, although I was able to get them easily in the TIDY format using python, the data in the two charts did not match each other: for example, some countries only had GDP per capita data but no corruption index. Finally, I used the VLOOKUP function in Excel to match the data and then manually removed the countries and regions with blank data.

When working with the data on enrollment ratio, I needed to match the ratios with countries that had the Top 200 universities. I used the SUMIF and LOOKUP functions in Excel to query and match the data.

The data for enrollment ratio is from 1971 to 2014, however, the top 200 universities list is updated in 2021. To eliminate the year mismatch, I used Excel to get an average value of the enrollment ratio for each country.

Conclusion

The number of top 200 universities in the UK is evidence of the quality of higher education in the UK. Also, the bubble chart with the regression line shows that higher education has a positive correlation with GDP. This may suggest that the quality of higher education in the UK will lead to another positive trend in the UK economy. Also, the high corruption index in the UK implies that the UK has a clean political environment and by regressing we also realize that the UK's economic strength is positively related to its clean political environment. I believe that education is the future of a country and a clean political environment is the foundation of a stable economy. When a country is politically clean, the social costs will be minimal and there will be a virtuous cycle in the economy. Thus, although the UK economy may seem to be shrinking, the future and foundations of the country are still in place, so what is there to worry about?
